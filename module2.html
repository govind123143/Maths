Large Sample Tests Hypothesis Testing · z-Test · Confidence Intervals

1
Define large sample test. When is it applicable?
CO 2L33 M

A large sample test (z-test) is used when sample size \(n \geq 30\). By the Central Limit Theorem, the sampling distribution is approximately normal.

\[Z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \sim N(0,1)\]

Applicable when: \(n \geq 30\), population SD known, testing means/proportions. Critical values: ±1.96 (5%), ±2.576 (1%) for two-tailed tests.

2
Explain i) Type-I and Type-II error ii) Hypothesis.
CO 2L33 M

Hypothesis

  • Null Hypothesis (H₀): Assumes no difference/effect. Tested for rejection.
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): Contradicts H₀; accepted if H₀ is rejected.

Type-I Error (α)

Rejecting H₀ when it is actually true. Also called false positive. Probability = level of significance α.

Type-II Error (β)

Failing to reject H₀ when it is false. Also called false negative. Power of test = 1 − β.

DecisionH₀ TrueH₀ False
Reject H₀Type I Error (α)Correct (Power)
Accept H₀CorrectType II Error (β)
3
What is meant by 5% and 1% level of significance?
CO 2L33 M

The level of significance (α) is the probability of committing a Type-I error.

  • 5% level (α=0.05): H₀ rejected if probability of result < 5%. Critical value: ±1.96. 5% risk of false rejection.
  • 1% level (α=0.01): Stricter. H₀ rejected only if probability < 1%. Critical value: ±2.576. 1% risk of false rejection.

Lower significance → stricter test → less Type-I error, but higher risk of Type-II error.

4
In 324 throws of a die, odd number turned up 181 times. Is the die unbiased?
CO 2L33 M

H₀: p=0.5 (unbiased). H₁: p≠0.5.

  1. \(n=324,\;\hat{p}=181/324=0.5586\)
  2. \(Z=\dfrac{0.5586-0.5}{\sqrt{0.25/324}}=\dfrac{0.0586}{0.02778}=2.11\)
  3. \(|Z|=2.11>1.96\)
Reject H₀: The die is biased at 5% level of significance.
5
Define i) Critical Region ii) One-tailed and Two-tailed test.
CO 2L33 M

Critical Region

The set of values of the test statistic for which H₀ is rejected. If the computed statistic falls here, reject H₀.

One-tailed Test

Tests if parameter is greater than or less than hypothesised value. Rejection region in one tail. E.g., H₁: μ > μ₀.

Two-tailed Test

Tests if parameter is different from hypothesised value. Rejection region split between both tails. E.g., H₁: μ ≠ μ₀. At 5%: |Z| > 1.96.

7
Population: 2,3,6,8,11. All samples of size 2 with replacement. Find population mean and SD.
CO 2L33 M
  1. N=5, Population: 2,3,6,8,11
  2. \(\mu=\frac{2+3+6+8+11}{5}=\frac{30}{5}=6\)
  3. Squared deviations: 16, 9, 0, 4, 25. Sum=54
  4. \(\sigma^2=54/5=10.8,\quad \sigma=\sqrt{10.8}\approx3.286\)
Mean = 6  |  SD ≈ 3.286
8
18000 families; sample of 840 showed 206 with income ≤ Rs.2500. Estimate how many of 18000 have income ≤ Rs.2500 with confidence limits.
CO 2L37 M
  1. \(n=840,\;\hat{p}=206/840=0.2452,\;\hat{q}=0.7548\)
  2. \(\text{SE}=\sqrt{\hat{p}\hat{q}/n}=\sqrt{0.2452\times0.7548/840}=0.01484\)
  3. 95% CI: \(0.2452\pm1.96\times0.01484=0.2452\pm0.0291\)
  4. Limits: 0.2161 to 0.2743
  5. Families: \(18000\times0.2161=3890\) to \(18000\times0.2743=4937\)
Between 3890 and 4937 families have income ≤ Rs.2500 (95% CI)
9
1500 ball bearings: μ=6.35g, σ=1.36g. 300 samples of size 36. Find expected mean and SD i) with replacement ii) without replacement.
CO 2L37 M

\(N=1500,\;\mu=6.35,\;\sigma=1.36,\;n=36\)

i) With Replacement
  1. Expected mean of sample means \(=\mu=6.35\)
  2. SE \(=\sigma/\sqrt{n}=1.36/\sqrt{36}=1.36/6=0.2267\)
Mean=6.35, SE=0.2267
ii) Without Replacement
  1. SE \(=\dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\sqrt{\dfrac{N-n}{N-1}}=0.2267\times\sqrt{\dfrac{1464}{1499}}=0.2267\times0.9883=0.2241\)
Mean=6.35, SE≈0.2241 (without replacement)
10
Sample of 500 men: 60% overweight. Infer proportion of overweight in population.
CO 2L37 M
  1. \(n=500,\;\hat{p}=0.60,\;\hat{q}=0.40\)
  2. SE \(=\sqrt{0.6\times0.4/500}=0.02191\)
  3. 95% CI: \(0.60\pm1.96\times0.02191=0.60\pm0.04294\)
95% CI: (55.7%, 64.3%) — population proportion of overweight
11
Company A: n₁=100, x̄₁=1190, s₁=90. Company B: n₂=75, x̄₂=1200, s₂=120. Test difference in mean bulb lifetime at 5% and 1%.
CO 2L37 M

H₀: μ₁=μ₂. H₁: μ₁≠μ₂.

  1. SE \(=\sqrt{s_1^2/n_1+s_2^2/n_2}=\sqrt{8100/100+14400/75}=\sqrt{81+192}=\sqrt{273}=16.52\)
  2. \(Z=(1190-1200)/16.52=-0.605\)
  3. |Z|=0.605 < 1.96 (5%) and < 2.576 (1%)
Accept H₀: No significant difference in mean lifetimes at both 5% and 1% levels.
12
Company A: n₁=1000, x̄₁=50, σ₁=15. Company B: n₂=1500, x̄₂=45, σ₂=20. Do mean wages vary? Find 95% confidence limits for difference.
CO 2L37 M

H₀: μ₁=μ₂. H₁: μ₁≠μ₂.

  1. SE \(=\sqrt{225/1000+400/1500}=\sqrt{0.225+0.267}=\sqrt{0.492}=0.7\)
  2. \(Z=(50-45)/0.7=7.14\)
  3. |Z|=7.14 >> 2.576
Reject H₀: Mean wages differ significantly.
95% CI: \((50-45)\pm1.96\times0.7=5\pm1.372 = (3.628,\;6.372)\)
95% CI for difference in mean wages: (3.628, 6.372)
13
Overall mean=74.5, SD=8. One school with 200 students: mean=75.9. Discuss significance (one-tailed and two-tailed).
CO 2L37 M
  1. \(Z=\dfrac{75.9-74.5}{8/\sqrt{200}}=\dfrac{1.4}{0.5657}=2.474\)

Two-tailed test: |Z|=2.474 > 1.96 → Significant at 5%; < 2.576 → Not significant at 1%.

One-tailed test: Z=2.474 > 1.645 → Significant at 5%; Z > 2.326 → Significant at 1%.

Result: School's mean is significantly higher (one-tailed at 1%, two-tailed at 5%).
14
Aircraft type A: 5 engine troubles in 100 flights. Type B: 7 in 200 flights. Is there a significant difference at 5%?
CO 2L37 M

H₀: p₁=p₂. H₁: p₁≠p₂.

  1. \(\hat{p}_1=5/100=0.05,\;\hat{p}_2=7/200=0.035\)
  2. Pooled: \(\hat{p}=(5+7)/(100+200)=12/300=0.04\)
  3. SE \(=\sqrt{0.04\times0.96(1/100+1/200)}=\sqrt{0.04\times0.96\times0.015}=\sqrt{0.000576}=0.024\)
  4. \(Z=(0.05-0.035)/0.024=0.015/0.024=0.625\)
  5. |Z|=0.625 < 1.96
Accept H₀: No significant difference between the two types of aircraft at 5% level.
15
1000 students each from city A and city B. 400 from payment quota in each sample. Is there a significant difference between cities?
CO 2L37 M

H₀: p₁=p₂. Both proportions = 400/1000 = 0.4.

  1. \(\hat{p}_1=\hat{p}_2=0.4\), so \(\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2=0\)
  2. SE \(=\sqrt{0.4\times0.6(1/1000+1/1000)}=\sqrt{0.24\times0.002}=\sqrt{0.00048}=0.02191\)
  3. \(Z=0/0.02191=0\)
Accept H₀: No significant difference between the two cities in payment quota students.
