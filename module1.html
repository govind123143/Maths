Probability Distributions Binomial · Poisson · Normal · Exponential

1
State the probability density function of the exponential distribution and derive its mean and standard deviation.
CO 1L33 M

PDF of Exponential Distribution

A continuous random variable \(X\) follows an exponential distribution with parameter \(\lambda > 0\):

\[ f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x},\quad x \geq 0 \qquad f(x)=0,\quad x<0 \]

Derivation of Mean

  1. \(E[X] = \int_0^{\infty} x \cdot \lambda e^{-\lambda x}\,dx\)
  2. Integration by parts: \(= \lambda\left[-\frac{x}{\lambda}e^{-\lambda x}\Big|_0^\infty + \frac{1}{\lambda}\int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda x}dx\right]\)
  3. \(= \lambda \cdot \frac{1}{\lambda^2} = \frac{1}{\lambda}\)
Mean \(= \dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)

Derivation of Standard Deviation

  1. \(E[X^2] = \int_0^\infty x^2 \lambda e^{-\lambda x}dx = \dfrac{2}{\lambda^2}\)
  2. \(\text{Var}(X) = \dfrac{2}{\lambda^2} - \dfrac{1}{\lambda^2} = \dfrac{1}{\lambda^2}\)
  3. \(\sigma = \sqrt{\text{Var}(X)} = \dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)
Standard Deviation \(= \dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)
2
Define Binomial distribution and write its probability mass function (PMF).
CO 1L33 M

A random variable \(X\) follows a Binomial Distribution \(B(n,p)\) if it counts the number of successes in \(n\) independent Bernoulli trials, each with probability of success \(p\).

\[ P(X = r) = \binom{n}{r} p^r (1-p)^{n-r},\quad r = 0,1,2,\ldots,n \]
  • Mean: \(\mu = np\)
  • Variance: \(\sigma^2 = npq\) where \(q = 1-p\)

4
Write the mean and variance of Poisson distribution.
CO 1L33 M

For a Poisson distribution with parameter \(\lambda\):

\[ P(X=r) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^r}{r!},\quad r=0,1,2,\ldots \]
  • Mean: \(E[X] = \lambda\)
  • Variance: \(\text{Var}(X) = \lambda\)

The unique property: mean equals variance for the Poisson distribution.

5
What is standard normal distribution?
CO 1L33 M

A standard normal distribution is a normal distribution with mean \(\mu=0\) and standard deviation \(\sigma=1\).

\[ \phi(z) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z^2/2},\quad -\infty < z < \infty \]

Any normal variate \(X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)\) is converted using:

\[ Z = \frac{X-\mu}{\sigma} \]

The curve is symmetric about \(z=0\). Critical values: \(Z_{0.05}=1.96\), \(Z_{0.01}=2.576\).

7
Obtain the mean and variance of the binomial distribution.
CO 1L314 M

Derivation of Mean

  1. \(E[X]=\sum_{r=0}^n r\binom{n}{r}p^r q^{n-r}\)
  2. Use \(r\binom{n}{r}=n\binom{n-1}{r-1}\): \(E[X]=np\sum_{r=1}^n\binom{n-1}{r-1}p^{r-1}q^{n-r}\)
  3. Substitute \(s=r-1\): \(=np(p+q)^{n-1}=np\)
Mean = np

Derivation of Variance

  1. Find \(E[X(X-1)]=\sum r(r-1)\binom{n}{r}p^rq^{n-r}\)
  2. Using \(r(r-1)\binom{n}{r}=n(n-1)\binom{n-2}{r-2}\): \(E[X(X-1)]=n(n-1)p^2\)
  3. \(E[X^2]=n(n-1)p^2+np\)
  4. \(\text{Var}(X)=n(n-1)p^2+np-n^2p^2=np-np^2=npq\)
Variance = npq
8
Obtain the mean and variance of the Poisson distribution.
CO 1L314 M

Mean

  1. \(E[X]=\sum_{r=0}^\infty r\cdot\frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^r}{r!}=e^{-\lambda}\sum_{r=1}^\infty\frac{\lambda^r}{(r-1)!}\)
  2. Let \(s=r-1\): \(=e^{-\lambda}\lambda\sum_{s=0}^\infty\frac{\lambda^s}{s!}=e^{-\lambda}\lambda e^\lambda=\lambda\)
Mean = λ

Variance

  1. \(E[X(X-1)]=e^{-\lambda}\lambda^2\sum_{s=0}^\infty\frac{\lambda^s}{s!}=\lambda^2\)
  2. \(E[X^2]=\lambda^2+\lambda\)
  3. \(\text{Var}(X)=(\lambda^2+\lambda)-\lambda^2=\lambda\)
Variance = λ  (Mean = Variance)
