Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) One-Way · Two-Way · Latin Square · ANCOVA

1
State the Principles of ANOVA.
CO 4L33 M
  • Replication: Each treatment is repeated several times to estimate experimental error and improve reliability.
  • Randomisation: Experimental units are assigned to treatments randomly to eliminate systematic bias.
  • Local Control: Units are grouped into homogeneous blocks to minimise variation from extraneous sources and improve precision.
2
Write any three applications of ANOVA.
CO 4L33 M
  1. Agriculture: Comparing crop yields under different fertilizers, irrigation methods, or seed varieties.
  2. Manufacturing / Quality Control: Comparing output or defect rates of machines from different manufacturers.
  3. Medical Research: Testing effectiveness of multiple drug treatments on patient outcomes simultaneously.
3
Explain the meaning of ANOVA. Describe the technique of analysis of variance.
CO 4L33 M

ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) is a statistical technique used to compare means of three or more groups by analysing the variance within and between groups. It tests H₀: all group means are equal.

Technique: Total variation (TSS) is partitioned into:

\[TSS = SS_{Between} + SS_{Within}\]

The F-ratio tests whether between-group variance is significantly greater than within-group variance:

\[F = \frac{MS_{Between}}{MS_{Within}} = \frac{SS_{Between}/(k-1)}{SS_{Within}/(N-k)}\]

If F > F_critical, reject H₀: at least one mean is different.

4
What is meant by between-group and within-group variations?
CO 4L33 M

Between-Group Variation (SS_Between)

Variability between treatment group means and the grand mean. Large value → treatments have a real effect.

\[SS_{Between}=\sum_j n_j(\bar{x}_j-\bar{\bar{x}})^2\]

Within-Group Variation (SS_Within / SSE)

Variability within each group due to random error. Used as the baseline (error) variance.

\[SS_{Within}=\sum_j\sum_i(x_{ij}-\bar{x}_j)^2\]

F-ratio = MS_Between / MS_Within. Large F → significant treatment effect.

6
Define ANCOVA. Differentiate between ANOVA and ANCOVA.
CO 4L33 M

ANCOVA combines regression and ANOVA. It adjusts for the effect of a continuous covariate before comparing group means.

ANOVAANCOVA
Compares group meansCompares adjusted means
No continuous covariateIncludes covariates
Pure experimentalCombines experimental + regression
May ignore confoundersControls for confounders
E.g.: Test 3 drug effectsE.g.: Test effects adjusted for age
